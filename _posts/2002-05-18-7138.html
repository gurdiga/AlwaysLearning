I think I'm finally getting it!

If you remember my last post, I was asking about putting together a
schedule for games and movies, etc. The consensus was not to make a
schedule because it would limit the possibilities. The other thing I
got from what people were saying was to learn what I want to learn
for ME and stop worrying about what my children are learning or *not*
learning.

At the time of my post, I had already gotten about 20 movies out of
the library. Days went by and I could not find the right time to
show Allison and Virginia the movies. I felt a little frustrated and
then it dawned on me, if I want to watch the movies, then I should
watch the movies for ME, *when* I want to watch them.

Well, I watched a few of the movies, and now I am very excited about
the possibilities of watching more movies, and learning more things,
for ME! It is very liberating to feel I no longer have to do things
because they are "educational" for the kid's benefit. I can do
things because *I* want to learn or because *I* am interested and
unschooling will happen.

I am surprised at how much I am beginning to be interested in.
