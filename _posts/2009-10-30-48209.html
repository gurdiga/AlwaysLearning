Heather at 2009-10-30 15:03:15 +0200 said:

I thought this was pretty interesting and that some people might enjoy
seeing it. My son sent this email to his principal yesterday when he
decided to stop going to 10th grade. She had asked if he would be
willing to meet with her to get feedback, and he decided that sending an
email would be an better way to express himself. He has been unschooling
until this September, and decided on his own to try this charter school
this fall. I told him I would leave out identifying info when I sent it
(not trying to protect the school.) He did this on his own. Usually he
asks me to review anything he has written before showing it to another
adult, and I was expecting that, but then he just sent me this, and said
he had sent it already.

Heather in(NY)



So, as you probably have heard I am leaving (name of school) and being
homeschooled again.
My decision isn't a reflection on the quality of the teachers or
curriculum at (name of school).

I didn't like that some of the other students would disrupt the learning
process sometimes.
There's probably going to be some degree of that in any school though.
Right?

I am an individual that has a good idea of how I want to learn, how I
want to spend my time, and what I want to focus on.
High school doesn't really work so well for me.
There is a set course of learning set out for me and I don't have as
much of a say in it as I would like to.

I think overall it was a positive experience to try school for a short
while.
Even if it was just to see what it was like.
