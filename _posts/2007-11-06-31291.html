6 Nov 2007, at 7:21am
We enjoyed www.bigfishgames.com. There are tons of games to choose from
and you can play each game for an hour free to see if you want to own
it. Also, if you agree to buy a certain number of games over 6 months
or a year, the price is pretty reasonable. Some of our favorites
include Insaneaquarium, Wonderland Adventures and Snailmail.

We also like Club Penguin and Dragon Fable. Depending on the age of
your children, Webkinz is also fun (you need to purchase a Webkinz
stuffed animal to get the code to play.) The animals run from $10 -
$15.00 and each pet gives you a one year membership to the site.

My 11 year old daughter loves Furcadia--it's an rpg. She could spend hours there--in fact it's fun, safe, and very educational (perhaps not intentionally, lol). Through this game she has been inspired to learn to do computer graphics and art, her verbal and written skills have zoomed to the point that her descriptive writing is beautiful and rich, a wonder to behold. It's totally free, too.

<<I like Bookworm Adventures, but it is definitely for readers
Robyn L. Coburn>>

Not in our house! My 8 yo liked the battle element of it, so I'd sit
and find words for him, easy at first, and he'd try spelling them (I
tried to find phonetic words) and I'd help however he wanted,
sometimes telling him the letters, sometimes saying sounds slowly,
sometimes pointing and clicking. He's on his way to reading and
writing this way, now sounding out names of items he picks up in
World of Warcraft. Not something I planned, but what worked cause he
liked the graphics! (And not that I'm endorsing phonics - it's just
what worked for him.)

Oh, another online game I recently learned of and thought was way
cool was http://linerider.com/ . Does anyone know if the physics of
that is accurate?

Pam
