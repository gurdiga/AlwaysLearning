Congrats to your daughter! We just made the switch to vegetarianism
not long ago (our son chose to continue eating meat since he
says "real men need meat"! lol) and we feel really good.

The biggest advice I would say is to avoid too much soy/tofu,
especially at her young age. Soy can mimic estrogen in the body and
may throw her hormones out of wack. If she starts to crave protein,
go for beans, seeds, or Ezekial bread instead. Or if she's not
avoiding dairy, it has good protein as well.

Have you checked out vrg.com? I don't do a lot of research on it but
that site seems to be less scare tactics with good info as well as
recipes.

My very favorite cook book of all times is "Passionate Vegetarian" by
Crescent Dragonwagon. It is like a novel about food. At 1,110 pages
long (seriously!) it is a wealth of knowledge. Even if you aren't a
vegetarian, this book is awesome as she goes into great details about
food and how its grown, different varieties, how to pick them, how to
cook them, etc. The way she talks about food you would swear she was
talking about sex! lol It will stir up the passion for food in
anyone and believe it or not, I have actually read the cookbook
cover to cover.
