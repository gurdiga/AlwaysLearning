We made our own using those shiny brass colored nails and a chunk of
wood. It is about one foot square and we measured and drew lines
every inch. The boys took turns pounding in the nails. I gave them a
pile of rubber bands and they use it like any other toy/game. Right
now it is in the closet for them to access as they like. We always
have lots of rubber bands to make rubber band balls - different colors
and sizes make it interesting.

We have three inexpensive blue plastic two-sided ones. One side has a
round design in the pegs, and the other is a grid.

It's not so important what you get (though that round side is pretty
cool). Get more than one kind, if you find another one later.

Different colors and sizes of rubber bands is an important thing to
consider.

here's a link to make one-its easy really.

All it takes to make a geoboard is a small square piece of wood, 25 nails, and a hammer. Draw five equally spaced vertical lines and five equally spaced horizontal lines. Hammer a nail at each of the 25 intersection points and, you have a geoboard.

To use it, however, you need one other item--rubber bands.
