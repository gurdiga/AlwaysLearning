The HSC conference in Sacramento was exhausting, but very fun. I
didn't take a camera. If anyone here has photos in a public place I
could see, that would be very cool.

Kirby was there. He ran a workshop on games based on history, and he
was on a young adult homeschoolers' panel.

I did three talks and was on two panels. (Hey, maybe that's why I'm
so tired!) They're for sale on CD, but I don't know the details yet
(and anyone who does is welcome to put that here).

Pam Sorooshian was there with Rose and Roxana.

While we were there, we went to see Stardust, which was highly
recommended by Marty (and others).
When Keith, Holly and Marty picked me and Kirby up at the airport,
they had the plan to go see Stardust. Kirby had already promised a
couple of friends he would meet with them (separately, at different
times), so we picked our friend Bo up when he got off work (same time
as our flight and not far from there), went to Dion's, took Kirby
home, and went to see Stardust.

Today I will put up a new page which isn't there yet containing the
questions asked on paper at the unschooling panel. We only got to
about half of them. And the other panelists will be contacted so
they can write answers to any that interest them particularly, if
they want to. So the conference isn't over for me yet. Oh! and
then there's laundry and paper and such--sorting the games out and
deciding which go with Kirby to Texas tomorrow and which stay here.

Kirby to Texas tomorrow...
