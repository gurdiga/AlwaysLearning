I love when that happens.

Years ago we went to a production of Little Shop of Horrors at Menaul
High School. They have an OLD gymnasium, 80 or 100 years old. It
was only half full or so, and I had taken lots of kids and we met up
with others. The back row was Kirby and Marty and other friends, so
they had a good view of the thing that happened. A friend named
Megan was young--fiveish--and sitting with me and Holly in the third
or fourth row back, or so. She didn't know the story. When Mr.
Mushnik leaned over to give the plant some water, Megan sat up
sharply and said (to the actor, that loudly) "NO! DON'T!" and it did
add greatly to the performance. "Mr. Mushnik" paused, and looked at
Megan thoughtfully and then went back to watering the plant with a
posture that said "Going to do it anyway." It was SO COOL that a
teenaged amateur actor in a high school show was able to incorporate
that unexpected outburst into what he was doing.



Sandra
