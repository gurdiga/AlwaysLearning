many cases that's the simple truth.

25 Jun 2007, at 11:19pm
Thank you for this discussion. I have been having this problem with
my 4 year old girl. I am trying really hard to not shame her, but I
do have a big reaction. For me, I think I have trouble because I
don't know how much of it bothers me because she was molested almost
a year ago and how much is just me being a very "normal" bothered
because it's inappropriate. I tell her that it's her body, but she
can't play with herself in front of anybody. I tell her that in
private it's fine. It's hard to know if saying something is private
is shaming or not. Child molesters sometimes say things like 'let's
keep this private' and such. I really don't know how strong my
reaction comes across to her, when she touches herself I am overcome
with grief over what happened to her. It's hard not to think that
one is related to the other. It's good to read that it's fairly
normal and that it's good to have a fairly normal "that's
innapropriate" reaction.
I don't want to teach her that bodies are bad in anyway. Well, this
is pretty choppy writing, but it's so emotionally charged that I have
some trouble with it. So, thanks for all the posts. It has helped
me a lot.


~Amy
