sheeboo2 at 2012-01-27 21:08:07 +0200 said:

I'm not sure I understand what you're asking--are you looking for ideas for snack foods to put out for your children in the evenings?

tea sandwiches are fun. Just make their favorite sandwiches and cut them into 1/4s.
nuts and seeds. We buy raw sunflower and pumpkin seeds and toss them with tamari and then roast them in the oven. Yummy
cut up cheese, fruit, vegetables, chicken/meat, marinated tofu
celery with peanut or other nut butters
mini muffins
dips: hummos, bean, queso, guacamole.....
nachos: corn chips with beans, cheese and guacamole
we make a lot of smoothies too, and they're often the foundation of Noor's main proteins. Right now, she's loving coconut milk, almond butter and chocolate sauce, but often they're yogurt, fruit, something sweet like maple syrup and maybe even some vegetables like spinach or kale. If you have bananas that are getting mushy, slice them and put them in the freezer. They're great in smoothies and add sweetness and act as a thickener.
This site has great recipes: http://www.smoothieweb.com/
