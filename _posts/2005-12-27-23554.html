I'm going to quote the germ of that statement:

-=-I am always scared-=-

Breathe deep breaths in and out when you think of it, and try to
think of it more often.

Massachusetts does have more laws than any two states put together
need, but there are lots of people there doing this, and there will
be a conference in Peabody in May. You should hook up with those
people and plan to go! (See the first link above.)

Actually the case law isn't too bad it's just that each town can make
it's own interpretation. And if there's just one homeschooler in a
town it can feel intimidating to tell the authorities that they're
asking too much.

The two secular state groups are:

Massachusetts Home Learning Association
http://mhla.org/

Advocates for Home Education in Massachusetts
http://www.ahem.info/

They both have articles on how to approach the case laws. I know MHLA
has an emai list. Not sure about AHEM.

There's also

My Dh and his sis are both ps teachers and she is very involved with Jocelyn have kinda been those voices. What I do to allay questions is that every few weeks I enthusiastically tell her what Jocelyn has been doing...such as oh...she's been writing her numbers to 100 and beyond and we are reading Peter Pan and she begs for it...and we are doing tons of phonics and usually sil is very satisfied.
