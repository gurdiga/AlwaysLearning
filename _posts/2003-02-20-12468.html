Do you have any things you especially like that I could put on the website?
I think if there's a repository of peaceful, soothing, encouraging (yet
smart-ass and pointed) things people can get to they won't have to suffer the
possibility of witnessing an argument or seeing a slow day at
www.unschooling.com.

The pace of acquiring information about unschooling is down from a year to
about half an hour now, with the internet and unschoolig.com. So rather than
just tell them to get a patient life and that in the old days we had to wait
two months in the snow for Growing Without Schooling, and it could be read in
full before the baby woke up... Now I've just decided to give them QUALITY
quick-fix unschooling information!!

There's a good discussion this week on unschooling.com (the forum) where so
meone asked about not having time to spend with her kids because she has to
get the house clean first, and I suggested she spend as much time with her
kids as they would spend in school, and that spooked her, and there was some
dust and whimpering and then it turned really sweet and wonderful. Anne O,
that show-off, helped as usual.

Ah. Speaking of Anne, in case there's anyone here who's not at
unschooling-dotcom or unschooling.com (list or forum), here's something new:

http://sandradodd.com/specialunschooling

That page is so far boring, but the three new articles (by Anne Ohman, Mary
Gold and Debbie Smith) are NOT boring.
