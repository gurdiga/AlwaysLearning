And if you're planning on attending the SC Live& Learn Unschooling
conference, you'll be RIGHT smack dab in the middle of the Revolutionary War.
Camden (with its revolutionary war parks, reinactments, museums, and stuff)
is only a 30 minute drive from my house. The character Gibson played was
loosely based on the Swamp Fox, Francis Marion---local hero. Tarleton

chocked-FULL of "colorful" characters! <g>

From www.teachwithmovies.org: (Recommended ages are in parentheses)

The Colonial Period
Ben & Me (U. S./Colonial Period & Pennsylvania; Science-Technology;
Biography; Char. Dev.: Male Role Model; Ethical Emphasis: Responsibility and
Citizenship) [6-9]
The Crucible (Drama; U.S./Colonial Period, 1945-1991 & Massachusetts; Char.
Dev.: Justice; Marriage; Ethical Emphasis: Trustworthiness and Fairness)
(Two versions, one in French with English Subtitles) [12+]
The Mayflower Voyagers [7 - 11]
Roots Vol. I (U.S./Diversity; Char. Dev.: Diversity) [12+]
Roots Vol. II (U.S./Diversity; Char. Dev.: Diversity) [12+]

1750 - 1812: Creating the Nation
1776 (U.S./Creating the Nation & Pennsylvania; Drama/Musicals; Char. Dev.:
Rebellion; Ethical Emphasis: Trustworthiness; Responsibility; Citizenship)
[11+]
The Birth of the Constitution (Char. Dev.: Patriotism) [9 - 12]
The Midnight Ride of Paul Revere (Literature; Char. Dev.: Patriotism;
Rebellion) [8-13]
Roots Vol. III (U.S./Diversity; Char. Dev.: Diversity) [12+]
The Smithsonian and the Presidency (U.S./1860 - 1865; and 1929 - 1941; Char.
Dev.: Patriotism) [7 - 11]

I liked The Patriot too, but follow it up with "what's true and what's not"
afterward. Mel's character was based partly on Robert Marion, the Swamp Fox.
http://www.geocities.com/BourbonStreet/1786/swampfox.html
Believe it or not, I recognized part of the story from my old Childcraft
books!
