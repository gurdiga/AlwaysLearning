games (Life, Scrabble, Monopoly etc), but are looking for something

Some of our favorites:

Party games best played with 4+ people:  Quelf (hilarious!!!), Apples to Apples (or Apples to Apples Jr), Cranium (mix of trivia, pictionary, charades, and other party games)

Strategy:  Settlers of Catan.   The regular board game requires 3-4 people to play.  If you want 5-6 people to play, you need to get the regular game + an expansion set.  There are also expansion sets for the game itself to add to the gameplay.  We love getting together with friends to play this game.  When my husband Ryan and I are at home, we play the Settlers of Catan card game which is for 2 players.  I like the card game better than the board game, honestly.

Cards:  Zombie Fluxx.   Ryan and I play this frequently.  It's called Fluxx because the rules and goal of the game change while you're playing.  The zombies add extra fun in the form of anti-goals.  A similar deck is Monty Python Fluxx, if your kids prefer Monty Python to Zombies. :)

I'm sure I'm forgetting something.  These days we play Catan and Fluxx mostly...  (except with our kids, but we're still in Candyland there.)

Definitely check out BoardGameGeek.com.  It's a terrific site, although you can end up sinking a lot of time into it if you like games. :)  Games magazine also just published their "Games 100", which has some interesting looking titles.  One of the best things to do would be to shop at a local game store instead of a toy store, if your area has them. (we have Uncles Games and some independently owned stores around here that are great for carrying top game titles that toy stores usually don't have)

-Renee

One thing I love is that they break things up into categories (two
player, strategy, family games, etc.) and have recommendations in each

Reading your list of games, there is a big area of games that you

We played KUDUUK today at park day - it is a great game, really fun.
Simple - lots of interaction, strategic thinking enough for the more
serious game players, but not burdensome to those who are not so
serious.

I got it hanging in the toys section of the 99 cent store.

However, there are two versions - the original version sold at
Starbucks and is more sophisticated. The version I got was sold
elsewhere and the rules are simplified (the game was bought by Mattel
and they simplified it).

I've played both and both are fun. I like the more complicated version
better - but the simplified version is still really good and has the
advantage of being quicker to learn so it works well for family get-
togethers and other spur-of-the-moment game playing.

-pam
