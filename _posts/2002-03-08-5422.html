That's too much dread to overcome! If you look forward to it, you'll at
least break even.

Unless it's a really Christian conference, I know what to expect in
general--there will be experienced homeschoolers who are all calm and you can
borrow some of their energy, and some newcomers who are afraid, and you could
cheer them up, and lots of cool stuff to look at in the vendor room (and some
scary stuff to make you glad you're an unschooler), and as the weekend goes
on you'll know some people and be having a good time.

That's my experience. It's probably about the same at a Christian
conference, except starts with a prayer and more of the speeches would be by
men who were saying America needed to get back to its Christian purpose, and
the family was the model of the nation and of the church, and men were the
head of the family.
