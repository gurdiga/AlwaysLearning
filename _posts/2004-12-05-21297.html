Hey, maybe I should introduce myself!

Hello all!

Our family has been living and learning here in rural SC for the past 15 years. We have 4 ds ages 16, 13, 8 and 6. We've unschooled from the start via the writings of John Holt, Grace Lewellyn, John Taylor Gatto, Sandra Dodd, and others. (shyly waving at Sandra ;-) We just try to live the richest life we can and do what we love. We've only been connected to the net for about 3 years now, and have flown 'under the radar' until this year if you get my drift. (still cautious about saying that) Anyway...LOVE this list....been lurking about 6 months. My 2 teens are totally immersed in studying web design, so we have 2 computers going with 1 extra phone line between them. I jump on as I can and have narrowed my lists down to this one. It's my favorite, and I'd love to add a word or two once in awhile if ya'll don't mind. :-) Still learning list etiquette, so let me know if I mess up. You have some great advice bouncing around on here. I think John Holt would be proud. (BWG)

Well, it's a beautiful day and we all have (literally) cabin fever...so we're gonna hit the woods for a hike. Thanks for listening, and for writing!


Margie

To be able to play Halo 2 online you need to buy a kit called Xbox
Live and connect it to your broadband modem at 512KB speed. If your
internet speed is less than this it won't work properly because it's
very slow and freezes during the game. Hope that helps.
