I just wanted to add to these two threads.

First, on the famous Tampa vacation, I took the girls to the Tampa Aquarium.
We loved it!! Ila (2.5) is now saying, "I the manatee girl!" She's very
excited about these gentle animals (we even got a plastic model from the
machine that makes them as you wait). Of course, it was a weekday and there
were many school groups with nametags, uniforms and clipboards with
worksheets on them. So many children (about grades 1-3) were simply skimming
the info cards for the "right answers" and not even watching the
animals/fish. I was pointing out the cool stuff (otters, turtles, wading
birds, etc) in the native Florida section while trying not to let the girls
get away from me and/or get trampled by the herd. I even mentioned to one
teacher (who had her won clipboard with worksheet on it), "Don't you
sometimes wish you could just bring the kids here without the questions to
ask?" She said, "Yes, and I also wish we could spend more time here. It
takes longer to organize and get here and back to school than the time we get
inside looking." How sad!
