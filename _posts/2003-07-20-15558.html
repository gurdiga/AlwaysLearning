Isn't she just awesome? We are currently in love with the holiday song, which
right now the song title escapes me, but it's the one where the Pagan niece
calls the Christian Uncle and has dinner at his home during Winter
Solstice/Christmas.

Kass and Kree find so many questions prompted by her songs, by most music
overall. It can be breath-taking and heartbreaking all rolled into those
conversations, but I think I am most grateful that they truly listen to all the music
we enjoy and want to understand the perplexities of society because of some
great lyrics. Just Friday, while at another homeschooler's birthday party, we
(adults) discussed music and it's effects on our society. We, DH & I, do not
censor the music the girls listen to, but rather try to learn what it is the
singer/songwriters are trying to say with them. I knew I would get a few eyeballs
popping out when I said that during the party. Not everyone understands why we
don't censor their music as most parents I know do censor their kids music.
Of course I have difficulty from the unschooler's perspective, especially when
music is not "allowed" for some kids due to the lyrics, but I accept that not
everyone is willing to subject themselves to hear things they are convinced as
just negativity to sell records. It just isn't that way for us, we see that
most songs are a story and generally have something to be gained, even those
drenched in profanity. Kass & Kree don't understand such censorship yet, maybe
they never will, since they haven't been subjected to that and may never be.
