>The best kind of friend is fine with your tears.

This conversation reminds me of a conversation I had on the
phone with my oldest daughters boyfriend this past June.

My dh and I were on our way back home (Helena, MT) from
Seattle. Running late we called home from Spokane and one of
our son's said we had to call Amy, so we did.

She had just gotten back home after emergency surgery for a
tubal pregnancy, she was naturally crying, which was good.

I spoke with her boyfriend and asked him how he was, of course
he said he was fine!

Then I said to him, don't stop my daughter from crying, she
has to get this out. Hold her in your arms all night long and
listen to her cry. He said, okay.

When we got off the phone he apparently asked Amy how I knew
that he had been telling her to stop crying!

As humans we are not comfortable with tears and crying. Look
at how we treat newborn babies, plug their mouths with
pacifiers and patting their backs, saying Sshhh and rocking
them all to be quiet.

I'm all for rocking and snuggling, etc... but all my babies
were allowed to cry while I held them and listened.

There is an art to listening!
