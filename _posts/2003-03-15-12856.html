Hi All:

This list has been quiet lately... I went to the hairdressers yesterday and
while waiting decided to read a magazine. There was an article in there
about Michael Essany. He's 20 years old and has been putting on a nighttime
talk show (like Leno or Letterman) from his living room with his mum and
dads' help for a couple of years. He's in college now and Leeza Gibbons
(Hollywood person) got to hear about him and the cable station "E" are doing
a documentary on his for 13 weeks as to how he puts his show together.

As I was reading the article I was thinking to myself "wow what an
innovative kid he must have been to know he wanted to do this and to pursue
it".... I read a little further in the magazine and it turns out he's
homeschooled.... Yipeeeeeeeeeeee!!! I don't know that he was unschooled
because of course the press doesn't spend much time differentiating. They
said his mother now 52 quit her job to homeschool her son... I was just
saying to my DH "why does the press seem to have to make it sound like she
quit her job, bought a blackboard, a piece of chalk and some text books and
that's how he learned?"...

Have any of your seen articles in the main stream press about unschooling?

Dawn
Tucson, AZ (Who was thrilled to find out that Michael Essany was
homeschooled or unschooled)

rjhill241@aol.com at 2003-03-16 02:13:19 +0200 said:

My family and I saw Michael Essany on Oprah a month or so ago, homeschooling
was never mentioned on her show. Then, flipping through channels one night,
we saw his show being previewed on E. Luckily, it was coming up next, so we
decided to watch it. He had Kelley Rowland (singer from Destiny's Child, who
recently put out her solo debut which my children bought immediately and
learned every word to every song) on his night show. It was great watching
this kid and his go-get-em style. Not five minutes into it though, my hubby
looked at me and stated, "This kid had to be homeschooled! I wonder if they
unschooled."

I was suprised that the statement came from him first as I hadn't even
thought about it much. I don't know why, perhaps because I spend a couple
more hours a day with the kids; but then again he has always supported and
understood unschooling from the day I took them out of school and gleefully
said we weren't ever doing "curriculum" again. I initially thought he might
be worried, but if he was, he never said so, only that he trusted me and knew
our kids well enough to trust them too. It is funny though because when they
are moody or bickering he threatens school at home and quickly they snap out
of it. LOL

Anyway, just my 2 cents and an elated mom with a hubby that "gets it".

Rhonda
