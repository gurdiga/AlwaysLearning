Sandra Dodd at 2013-12-13 22:06:51 +0200 said:

The U.S. Amazon site has (in some irritating move) put seven copies of my book up for half price.

I wrote and asked them why, and asked them not to, and after several e-mails was told that it was just two copies that were "lost" from the inventory I sent them, and that they had reimbursed me. They sent the note, saying they had reimbursed me for two.

Now it lists seven.

It's odd, it's weird, but I would rather people who read Always Learning buy them than strangers at random. :-)

http://www.amazon.com/Big-Book-Unschooling-Sandra-Dodd/dp/0557181550/ref=sr_1_1?ie=UTF8&qid=1386964567&sr=8-1&keywords=big+book+of+unschooling

I was thinking of lowering the price to $20, to get my Amazon inventory moved, lest they charge me storage, because their rates have gone up for such direct sales. But Still, even at $20, nobody will buy one from me while they're selling it for $12.70.

It's listed as Amazon Prime, so if you need a Christmas gift for a new unschooling friend, there's that.

European Amazon outlets have a direct deal with lulu, where if you buy one in Europe they do a print-on-demand deal, so those are cheaper than mine, too. I ordered paper copies from Lulu and then shipped those in boxes to Amazon warehouses. BIG pain in the butt. And now Amazon does this tricksy little move. :-)

Sandra
