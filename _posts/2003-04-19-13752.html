Thanks, but Holly would have to be standing there pointing and telling me
what to cut while I said "Are you sure?"

Those Ponies came from thrift stores, except for the few her cousin, Gina,
gave her. Gina has a big collection, and is old enough to have gotten some
new, so she has some of the cardboard backings and that is Information.
Gina used to have a whole Pony culture in her room. As a new pony showed up,
it went to the audience chamber where it stayed with the queen of the ponies
and the other important pony-ages (personage? ponitude?) and at some point
it was sent to meet other ponies and find a home. They all had elaborate
braiding jobs.

Holly likes to curl hers with curlers, leave them on the deck to dry, and
such.

Back to hair! Maybe Holly will do hair. <g>

She got to help braid a horse's mane once. Ribbons and all.

The horse rolled around in the dirt that night. I got to help her fix the
mess the next day. She was having way more fun than I was.

Maybe she'll just do her own kids' hair, though, and their dolls and ponies
and her friends' horses!
