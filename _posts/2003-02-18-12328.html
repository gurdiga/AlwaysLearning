> I thought most people did corn mazes at night? Everyone we know does,
> anyway. Kind of like a haunted house...

Yikes, I wouldn't. They're dark and creepy enough in the daytime for me.

I did one before we left Washington state that was set up like the U.S., and
there were little open spots at each state. I was good at that one, you
just had to work your way across the country, and if you headed too far off
course, you knew immediately. Or at least *I* did. But there were plenty
of confused teenagers in there trying to remember if Kansas was north of
Nebraska or south, and if Ohio was east of the Missisippi or west. It was
funny. Sort of pitiful, but funny, too.

I still don't like corn mazes, though, but maybe that's because I find them
clausterphobic. Never seen them here in Germany; Tim doesn't remember them
now.
