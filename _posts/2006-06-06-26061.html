Joanne at 2006-06-07 00:54:29 +0300 said:

Hi Lesa (I answered you at UB also)

I moved to Florida (Ocala) from NYC in 2002.

There are 2 ways to homeschool in this state. One is through the
state and one is through a 600/non-public school.

Through the state, you have to submit a yearly portfolio and have
your child evaluated.
Through a 600 school, you follow the rules of that school. The only
procedure a 600 school is required by law to follow is that they
collect attendence (180 dates). If they have any other rules, those
are their own rules that they decided on.
I belong to AEI. Here is the link:
http://alternativeeducationinstitute.org/index.htm
The only requirement they have is collecting attendence, which you
send in through an e-mail. You can pass on the health
exam/immunization part for religious or moral reasons.
The woman who runs the school, also runs a very informative yahoo
group on Florida homeschooling laws. Here is the link:
http://groups.yahoo.com/group/FL-HomeEducation-Law/

Please feel free to contact me if you need more information!
