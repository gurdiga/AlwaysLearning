As a former Phoenix resident, I always recommend:

Hiking - Echo Canyon Park (west side of Camelback Mtn), a taste of Sedona in the center of Phoenix; Squaw Peak, hiking as social event; and South Mountain Park (16,000 acres)

Taliesin West - Frank Lloyd Wright's architecture school, still in operation, daily tours about how Wright integrated architecture, music, and the natural environment

Be sure to go look at the photos - otherwise this might not sound as
cool as it really seems to be. It is just a few minutes from the
conference site.

<http://www.dbg.org/index.php/chihuly>

Chihuly: The Nature of Glass
November 22, 2008 to May 31, 2009

The Desert Botanical Garden is excited to present an exhibition by
renowned artist Dale Chihuly. /Chihuly: The Nature of Glass/ will
feature new and unique works of glass artfully located throughout the
Garden. Dale Chihuly is known for his innovative glass sculptures, and
his work is immediately recognizable for its grand scale and vibrant
colors.

Chihuly's spectacular creations are found in more than 200 museums
worldwide including the Metropolitan Museum of Art, London's Victoria
and Albert Museum and the Smithsonian American Art Museum. Garden
exhibitions of his work have been mounted at the New York Botanical
Garden, Fairchild Tropical Botanic Garden, Atlanta Botanical Garden,
Missouri Botanical Garden, Phipps Conservatory and the Royal Botanical
Gardens at Kew. This is Chihuly's first exhibition in an outdoor desert
environment.
