How to play "A Case of You" by Joni Mitchell

What makes this an amazing coincidence is I listened to a bunch of
Joni Mitchell yesterday, including a recording Prince did of A Case
of You (which can be bought for 99 cents from iTunes and is pretty
wonderful). (Marty has been on a big Prince jag lately, but I
haven't shared that one with him because I don't think he knows the
Joni Mitchell version, and we've been really busy.)

This is the second amazing-coincidence accidental song find of the
day. It's not like I was looking through a bunch of YouTube stuff.
I was looking for a specific thing unrelated to either of them and
found them both.

The other was a version of "Take a Whiff," an old cocaine song I have
on a Cisco Houston album (33 rpm antique), which Holly and I wrote
verses for for a friend of ours who said once "Smell me--take a
whiff" because he had put cologne on. Longer funnier story, but
anyway... today I found something called "Take a Drink on Me" with
photos of pubs in Wales (and other mostly worthless stuff, and exotic
urinals at the end) and it was that very same song, which we first
sang for our friend last weekend.

WEIRD.

OH! And about "A Case of You," I'm very glad the guy made this.
It's very much like Blackbird, by Paul McCartney, which is also
played almost exclusively on the G and B strings! Guitarists will
appreciate this, and others might not mind it much.
